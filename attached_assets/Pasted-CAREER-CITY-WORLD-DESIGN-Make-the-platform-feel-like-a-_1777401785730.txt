CAREER CITY WORLD DESIGN:
Make the platform feel like a large 3D career city, not a flat app. The user enters a central Career City hub with roads, sidewalks, plazas, signs, buildings, and career districts.

Each career category should have its own corridor/district. Engineering must have a detailed Engineering Corridor with multiple buildings, including:
- Civil Engineering Building
- Mechanical Engineering Building
- Electrical Engineering Building
- Software/Computer Engineering Building
- Chemical Engineering Building
- Aerospace Engineering Building
- Environmental Engineering Building
- Biomedical Engineering Building
- Construction/Infrastructure Zone

Each building should contain hands-on 3D simulations tied to that specific field. Users should enter buildings and immediately perform interactive career tasks, not answer traditional quizzes.

CAREER ADVICE NPCs:
Add other people walking around the city who can give career advice. These NPCs should include:
- Professionals
- Mentors
- Students
- Recruiters
- Counselors
- Alumni-style guides

Users can walk up to them and ask questions about careers, skills, education paths, daily work, challenges, salaries, internships, and next steps. NPCs should use AI-generated dialogue and respond naturally.

MULTIPLAYER / SOCIAL SIMULATION MODE:
Make the platform accessible for users to complete simulations together online.

Users should be able to:
- Join the same 3D career city
- See other users as avatars
- Walk around together
- Enter simulations together
- Collaborate on tasks
- Talk through voice or text chat
- Invite friends/classmates into the same simulation
- Work as a team during certain scenarios

Examples:
- Engineering students collaborate to design and test a bridge
- Healthcare users work together in a hospital emergency scenario
- Business users pitch together in a startup boardroom
- Legal users roleplay attorney, witness, and jury roles
- Trades users troubleshoot a home repair scenario together

The world should support both:
- Solo simulation mode
- Multiplayer simulation mode

The experience should still keep users inside the 3D/VR world the entire time. Menus should appear only as in-world panels, floating holograms, city maps, building directories, or side HUD elements.
