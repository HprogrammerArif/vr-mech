3.8 AD ROTATION SYSTEM (15-SECOND CYCLE)

All digital ad surfaces (billboards, plaza displays, building-side ads, 
bus stop ads, kiosks) rotate creative every 15 seconds.

Rotation behavior:
- Each surface displays one ad for 15 seconds, then transitions to the 
  next ad in its rotation queue
- Smooth transition effect between ads (fade, slide, or flip — choose 
  per surface type for variety)
- Transitions take ~0.5 seconds; full ad visible for 14.5 seconds
- Multiple ads per surface supported (queue of 4-12 ads typical)
- Rotation continues whether or not players are looking — when a player 
  approaches, they see whichever ad is currently in the cycle
- Ads sync across all players on the same surface (everyone sees the 
  same ad at the same time — creates shared experience)

Server-side rotation logic:
- Master clock controls all ad surfaces globally
- Each surface has an independent rotation queue but shares the 15-second tick
- Ad rotations logged for impression tracking (each cycle = 1 impression 
  if player is within viewing distance and the ad is in their viewport)
- Different surfaces show different ads at the same time (city feels 
  varied, not synchronized)

Targeting within rotation:
- Rotation queues can be customized per district (Trades District 
  surfaces show trade-relevant ads more frequently)
- Time-of-day targeting (morning ads vs. afternoon ads)
- Audience-tier targeting (different ads for different grade levels 
  if appropriate)
- Premium advertisers can purchase guaranteed slot frequency 
  (e.g., "shown every 60 seconds" = 1 of every 4 rotations)

Static surfaces (non-rotating):
- Bench ads, small print ads, and "permanent sponsorship" placements 
  remain static (no rotation)
- District-naming sponsorships are static (e.g., "Trades District 
  presented by [Sponsor]" doesn't rotate)
- Only digital/animated surfaces rotate on the 15-second cycle

Performance considerations:
- Pre-load next 2 ads in queue to avoid loading delay during transition
- Cache ad creative in browser memory for active session
- Limit simultaneous animated ad transitions in viewport to maintain 
  frame rate
- Lower-priority ad surfaces (far from player) can pause rotation 
  to save resources

Impression analytics:
- Track which ad was on which surface during each 15-second window
- Track player viewport overlap with ad surface (was ad actually visible?)
- Distance from player to surface (closer = higher quality impression)
- Click-through events tied to specific ad creative + surface + timestamp
- Advertiser dashboard shows per-creative performance, not just per-campaign
