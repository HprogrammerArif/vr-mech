Enhance 1WayMirror World, the existing 3D career exploration platform for 
7th-12th grade students. The current state is functional but static. 
Add the following features to make the world more interactive, realistic, 
and engaging while maintaining the low-poly visual style and the existing 
safety-monitored chat system.

=== PRIORITY 1: AMBIENT LIFE & ATMOSPHERE ===

1.1 NPC POPULATION
- Add 15-25 ambient NPCs walking through the city
- NPCs follow simple paths between districts (waypoint navigation)
- Different NPC styles per district:
  * Trades District: workers in hard hats, tool belts
  * Science District: lab coats, clipboards
  * Healthcare District: scrubs, stethoscope models
  * Tech District: hoodies, laptop bags
  * Arts District: artistic outfits, sketchbooks
- NPCs play idle animations when paused (looking around, checking phone, 
  waving, drinking coffee)
- Click on an NPC to get a brief career fact or "Day in the Life" tooltip
- NPCs respect player space (move around player rather than through)

1.2 DYNAMIC ENVIRONMENT
- Day/night cycle (real-time or accelerated 20-minute cycle)
- Sun position affects lighting and shadows
- Street lamps turn on at dusk
- Building windows light up at night
- Distant ambient sounds: city hum, light traffic, occasional birds
- Subtle particle effects: leaves drifting, wind gusts moving trees
- Animated 1WayMirror World banners (subtle wave/sway)
- Flag movement on poles
- Fountains in plazas with animated water

1.3 WEATHER VARIATION (OPTIONAL, LOW PRIORITY)
- Light rain effect occasionally (with sound)
- Snow in winter months
- Sunny default

=== PRIORITY 2: INTERACTIVE BUILDINGS ===

2.1 BUILDING DETAILING
Replace placeholder boxes with detailed buildings featuring:
- Windows (some lit, some dark)
- Doors with district-appropriate styling
- Roof details (chimneys, antennas, signs)
- District-themed architecture:
  * Trades: warehouse-style, exposed beams, crane silhouettes
  * Science: modern glass, tall research towers
  * Healthcare: hospital-cross signage, clean white architecture  
  * Tech: sleek minimalist, holographic-style signs
  * Business: skyscraper-style, corporate logos
  * Arts: colorful, irregular shapes, mural exteriors
  * Education: classical columns, clock tower

2.2 HOVER INTERACTIONS
- Buildings glow with colored outline when player approaches (within 5m)
- Floating UI prompt appears: "Press E to Enter [Building Name]"
- Building name and career category shown above building (always visible 
  but subtle)
- Distance indicator on minimap

2.3 INTERIOR SPACES
Each major building has an interior:
- Smooth transition (fade or door animation)
- Themed interior matching the career
- Interactive objects inside (computer for tech, microscope for science, 
  blueprints for trades)
- NPC career mentor inside who can chat with student
- "Try the Career" mini-experience (simple interactive task)
- Exit clearly marked

=== PRIORITY 3: ADVERTISING & SPONSORSHIP INTEGRATION ===

The world should support multiple advertising surfaces as a monetization 
channel. Build out 1WayMirror World as a "real city" with ad space that 
companies, colleges, trade schools, and career programs can purchase. 
Existing 1WayMirror branded billboards remain — additional ad surfaces 
should be added throughout the world.

3.1 BILLBOARD NETWORK
Multiple billboard types throughout the city:
- Large highway-style billboards at city entrances (premium tier)
- Standard plaza billboards (currently exist with 1WayMirror branding — 
  expand network)
- Building-side wall ads (large surface area on tall buildings)
- Bus stop / transit shelter ads (eye-level, high traffic)
- Bench advertising (small format, high frequency)
- Stadium/arena perimeter ads (around event spaces)
- Animated digital displays in plazas (rotating ads, premium pricing)

3.2 IN-WORLD SPONSORSHIP OPPORTUNITIES
Beyond traditional billboards, integrate sponsorship into the experience:
- Sponsored buildings: "Tech District presented by [Sponsor]"
- Career booth sponsorships: a company sponsors the entire experience for 
  their industry (e.g., "Nursing Career Path sponsored by [Hospital System]")
- Branded NPCs: industry mentor NPCs can wear sponsor logos appropriately 
  (e.g., apprenticeship program rep wears their union/trade school colors)
- Sponsored events: career fairs, AMAs, scholarships announced via 
  in-world banners
- Branded objects: tools, books, equipment in interior spaces can carry 
  sponsor logos (subtle product placement, never feels forced)
- Scholarship kiosks: sponsored by colleges/foundations, lead to real 
  application info

3.3 AD CONTENT MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Build an admin-side ad management dashboard:
- Upload ad creative (images, optionally short video)
- Assign to specific surface types (billboard, wall, kiosk, etc.)
- Set rotation schedule (multiple ads cycle on same surface)
- Geographic/district targeting (Trades District ads in Trades only)
- Audience targeting (grade level, career interest tags)
- Campaign duration and pacing controls
- Click-through tracking (ads can be "interactive" — students click for 
  more info, opens info panel in-world, NEVER navigates outside platform)
- Impression and engagement analytics dashboard for advertisers
- Ad approval workflow (admin reviews before going live)

3.4 AD CONTENT POLICIES (CRITICAL FOR MINOR PLATFORM)
Strict rules enforced by admin review and AI content scanning:
- ONLY education, career, scholarship, college, trade school, military, 
  apprenticeship, and youth-appropriate brand advertising
- NO advertising for: alcohol, tobacco, vaping, gambling, dating apps, 
  weight loss products, financial products (loans, credit cards), 
  political content, adult content, or any age-restricted product
- NO ads featuring real adult celebrities directed at students
- All ad creative reviewed by AI moderation pipeline + human admin 
  before going live
- All ads must include clear sponsor identification (no disguised content)
- Ads cannot collect personal information from students directly — 
  click-through goes to information panels, parent-notified contact 
  request, or general public information only
- COPPA-compliant: no behavioral tracking of users under 13 for ad targeting

3.5 IDEAL ADVERTISER TYPES (PITCH TARGETS)
The platform's natural advertisers include:
- Trade schools and apprenticeship programs
- Community colleges and universities
- Military recruiters (with appropriate guidelines)
- Scholarship foundations
- Youth career programs (Junior Achievement, FFA, FBLA, etc.)
- Industry associations (electricians union, nursing association, etc.)
- Career-focused books/courses appropriate for teens
- Hospital systems recruiting future healthcare workers
- Tech companies with internship/apprenticeship programs
- Construction and skilled trades companies
- STEM education programs and camps

3.6 AD PLACEMENT TIERS & PRICING STRUCTURE
Build the system to support tiered pricing:
- Premium: Highway billboards, plaza digital displays, district sponsorships
- Standard: Building-side ads, bus stop ads, mid-tier billboards
- Entry: Bench ads, small kiosks, rotating display slots
- Native: Sponsored mentor NPCs, sponsored career path experiences
- Event: Time-limited sponsorships during career fairs, AMAs
Pricing logic supports: monthly flat rate, per-impression CPM, 
or per-click CPC models

3.7 ADVERTISER PORTAL
Separate login for advertisers to:
- Upload and manage their ad creative
- View campaign performance (impressions, clicks, engagement)
- See demographic breakdowns (aggregated, never individual student data)
- Pay invoices and renew campaigns
- Submit new creative for approval

=== PRIORITY 4: PLAYER ENGAGEMENT FEATURES ===

4.1 ONBOARDING & QUEST SYSTEM
- New player tutorial: Dario (the AI guide) appears at spawn
- Welcome quest: "Visit any 3 career districts to unlock your first badge"
- Quest log visible in HUD
- Mission markers on minimap
- Progressive unlock: complete quests to access new districts/features
- Daily challenges: "Talk to 2 NPCs today" / "Take a career assessment"

4.2 CAREER PROGRESSION SYSTEM
- Each student has a "Career Explorer" profile
- Visit buildings → earn XP for that career field
- Reach milestones → earn badges (Apprentice, Specialist, Expert)
- Badges visible on player profile and during interactions
- "Career DNA" generated over time based on visited buildings, 
  time spent, completed activities
- Career match score updates as student explores

4.3 INTERACTIVE OBJECTS IN WORLD
- Career assessment kiosks scattered throughout city
- "Day in the Life" video booths
- Job shadow simulators (mini-games for each career)
- Resume Builder station
- Mock Interview rooms with AI interviewer
- Career Fair area where multiple career NPCs gather
- Scholarship kiosks (sponsored, see Priority 3)

4.4 PERSONAL HUB / DORMITORY
- Each student has a personal space they can customize
- Trophy room showing earned badges
- Career portfolio displayed on walls
- Personal AI assistant (Dario) lives here
- Friends list and current online status
- Customizable avatar wardrobe

=== PRIORITY 5: SOCIAL & COLLABORATIVE FEATURES ===

5.1 SHARED SPACES
- Auditorium for live events (career speakers, AMAs)
- Group study rooms for collaborative learning
- Sports/games area for casual hangout (with safety monitoring)
- Career Fair plaza where students gather (prime sponsorship space)

5.2 SCHEDULED EVENTS
- Daily career spotlight (NPC speaker for 15 minutes)
- Weekly career fair with multiple industries
- Monthly competitions (resume contest, mock interview tournament)
- Live AMAs with real industry professionals (vetted adults only)
- Sponsored events from advertisers (e.g., "Tech Day brought to you by 
  [Sponsor]")

5.3 PEER INTERACTION (SAFETY-FIRST)
- Group activities require 3+ students (no isolated 1-on-1 by default)
- Project-based teams formed by AI matching (interests + grade level)
- Peer mentor program (12th graders mentoring 7th-8th graders, all 
  monitored)
- Public bulletin boards for sharing achievements (moderated)

=== PRIORITY 6: VISUAL POLISH ===

6.1 LIGHTING IMPROVEMENTS
- Soft global illumination
- Volumetric fog in distance (atmospheric depth)
- Building lights cast realistic glow at night
- Billboard and digital display lights cast appropriate glow
- Avatar shadows under feet
- Rim lighting on player character

6.2 ANIMATION UPGRADES
- Smooth walk/run cycles for player
- Idle animations when standing still (breathing, looking around)
- Wave/dance/sit emotes (E key opens emote wheel)
- Smooth camera movement with subtle bob during walking
- Animated transitions between buildings
- Animated digital billboards (videos and rotating ads)

6.3 UI/UX POLISH
- Larger, more legible district name signs
- Floating tooltip when hovering over interactive elements
- Improved minimap: clickable districts, friend dots, quest markers
- Quest tracker in corner
- Notification toast for achievements
- Smooth fade transitions between UI states
- Ad click → in-world info panel (never leaves platform)

6.4 SOUND DESIGN
- Ambient city soundscape (low volume background)
- Footstep sounds matching surface (concrete, grass, wood)
- UI sound effects (button clicks, menu opens, notifications)
- Achievement fanfare for milestones
- District-specific ambient music (low volume, optional toggle)
- Voice acting for Dario (or high-quality TTS)

=== PRIORITY 7: REALISM & IMMERSION ===

7.1 PHYSICS & FEEL
- Avatar has slight physics (body sway, head turn following look direction)
- Objects can be picked up and examined (clipboard, tools, books)
- Doors swing open with proper animation
- Camera collision detection (camera doesn't clip through walls)

7.2 SCALE & PROPORTION
- Adjust building heights to feel impressive but not overwhelming
- Add depth: foreground, midground, background elements
- Distant city skyline visible beyond playable area (background ad 
  surfaces possible here too)
- Mountains or natural features on horizon
- Skybox with realistic clouds

7.3 PERFORMANCE OPTIMIZATION
- LOD (level of detail) system for distant objects
- Occlusion culling for buildings out of view
- Instanced rendering for trees, lamp posts, NPCs
- Lazy-load ad creative based on player proximity
- Target 60fps on mid-range hardware
- Mobile-friendly fallback graphics mode

=== TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION NOTES ===

- Use Three.js or Babylon.js (already in your stack presumably)
- Implement ECS (Entity Component System) for NPCs and interactive objects
- Use GLTF format for 3D models (efficient, web-optimized)
- Animation via skeletal rigs (Mixamo for free animations to start)
- WebSocket for real-time multiplayer state sync
- Spatial audio for immersion (Howler.js or similar)
- Save player progress to existing user database
- Ad creative served via CDN for fast loading
- Ad impression tracking via WebSocket events (when ad enters viewport)
- All new chat/interaction features must route through existing 
  safety-monitoring system (do not bypass moderation pipeline)

=== SAFETY INTEGRATION ===

Every new interactive feature must:
- Respect existing trust score restrictions
- Pass through AI moderation for any chat/text
- Trigger parent notifications per existing rules
- Maintain audit logging
- Not introduce new private/unmonitored interaction surfaces

Advertising-specific safety:
- All ad creative reviewed by AI + human admin before going live
- Ad click-throughs stay within platform (no external redirects for minors)
- No personal data collection through ad interactions
- Parents can view what ads their student has interacted with
- Parents can opt their student out of ad personalization entirely
